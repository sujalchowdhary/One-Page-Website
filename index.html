COLDPLAY

ABOUT THE BAND

          Coldplay, British rock group whose melodic piano-driven anthems
          lifted it to the top of the pop music world in the early 21st century.
          Coldplay was formed in 1998 at University College, London, with the
          pairing of pianist-vocalist Chris Martin and guitarist Jonny Buckland.
          The band was later filled out with fellow students Guy Berryman on bass
          and Will Champion, a guitarist who later switched to drums. Coldplay
          penetrated the U.K. Top 100 in 1999 with the single “Brothers & Sisters”
          on the independent Fierce Panda label before signing with major label
          Parlaphone.
